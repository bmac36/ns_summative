Facts and Information

The majority of indigenous people in Guatemala are of Mayan descent and it is estmated that they take up 51% of the national population in Guatemala. Even so, there are many obstacles the indigenous people face with regards to getting an education.

The law of linguistics rights of indigenous people states "the federal educative authroities in mexico and in the states of the country will guarantee that the indigenous population has access to obligatory, bilingual, and intercultural education". (article 11, 2004)

The Obstacles They Face

  • 750,000 to 1,200,000 people speak Mayan, but three times that number come from Maya origina and are not capable to speak their native language.
  • Even with the large population that speaks Mayan, no bilingual or intercultural education exists past elementary school.
  • Because of the language barrier, many students that do get an education struggle to understand and require additional tutoring.
  • 83.4% of the Mayans 15+ will drop out before finishing junior year and only only 6.6% of Mayan people obtain education past that point.
  • In Yucatan alone there are only 256 elementary schools, 330 kindergarten, 98 preschools, and 50 boarding schools in 2006/07.
  • Out of the elementary schools, more than 33% closed down and 52 of the kindergarten schools closed down depsite them being a mandatory action children must go through.
  • In Maxcanu district, there are 3,325 kindergarden students — but only 283 students can proceed to primary schools.
  • There are not enough teachers trained to teach the Mayan curriculum.
  • Only 57% of teachers for indigenous primary education in Yucatan reached higher education themselves.
  • Only 37% of teachers have reached a bachelors degree.
  • There are many serious effects caused by the education policy failures:
    • large numbers of Mayan students who have completed primary school can't read or write in any language
    • discrimination runs rampant through the schools
    • children have low self-confidence, which exacerbates the inequality issues
    • the nature of the Mayan culture may be lost forever
  • Often the family can only afford to have one child go to school. Invariably a boy is chosen for this, so education for girls is even more of an issue.
