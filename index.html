The Issues at Hand

Article 14.2 of the UN declaration states:
"Indigenous individuals, particularly children, have the right to all levels and forms of education of the State without discrimination."

Unfortunately, more often than not, this is not happening because the cost of education is too high for the impoverished Mayan communities to afford. Even when a child does get the opportunity to attend school, it is usually a male of the family and he will have to leave as early as grade 6 because the family needs him to help with the farm or business.
